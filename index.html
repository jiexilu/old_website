Solar Car

University of Michigan Solar Car Team

I joined Solar Car Team my Freshman year at the University of Michigan, because I wanted to do something I’ve never done before and something I would be proud of looking back on my college career. My first day of Solar Car, I was thrown into the deep end almost immediately. I was learning how to design PCBs and helping out with functionality designs for the car. When I was accepted into Race Crew for the American Solar Challenge, I had been on the team for a little over 4 months.

For Race Crew, I was on the power electrical division and I stayed in Ann Arbor over the summer to work on the Solar Car. There, the division and I built the new battery pack, the new breaker system, and managed the overall wiring of the car to make it more efficient. On the race, my role was to drive the “Chase” vehicle, which followed closely behind the solar car and protected it from general traffic. The American Solar Challenge officials complimented me on my role as “Chase” driver after I prevented an aggressive driver from intercepting the solar car.

I enjoyed my experiences on Race Crew, because it taught me how to be a “team player.” There were a total of 20 members on Race Crew all from different majors, from art and design to all the engineering majors, and in different subdivisions of Race Crew. With all the different divisions on the team, I really learned how to work and communicate with different divisions, and help out in areas that were unrelated to my division. A perfect example of working with other divisions is building the battery pack for the solar car. I had to talk to the Mechanical division to get the battery box and the nickel tabbing constructed. I also had to work with the Micro Electrical division to lay out the battery monitoring system on the battery pack.

Aside from all of the work I did on Race Crew, I also made some of my closest friends on Solar Car. It’s amazing how I can walk into any classroom (related to my major or not) and find a person who is or was on the Solar Car team.
